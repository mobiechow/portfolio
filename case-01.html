Project Brief

A registered charity empowering wheelchair users and advocating for an accessible city. Working as in-house designer within an existing brand system — with limited production budget and a small team — I designed collateral that reflected the organisation's energy and initiated materials that improved the programme experience on the ground.

Design thinking

I proposed a circular name card format — a deliberate nod to the wheel at the heart of the organisation's identity, and a shape that immediately stands out in a pile of standard rectangles. Each card features a personalised Lego-style avatar chosen by the team member, turning a functional touchpoint into something people actually want to keep.

The pitch deck was built to take corporate sponsors on a journey — from the problem of inaccessible beaches, to the impact of Wheel to Beach, to a clear ask. I also initiated the accessibility observation guides after noticing a gap — giving participants a structured visual tool to carry during fieldwork.
